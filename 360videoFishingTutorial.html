360 Video Fishing Tutorial

In my second year of college, during the first semester, I worked on a project that involved VR and video editing. The assignment was designed to give us experience in both video editing and VR programming. Our group had members with varying skill sets, so we delegated tasks based on individual strengths.

We started by brainstorming ideas for the type of VR experience we wanted to create. We didn't just want the client to have a passive experience through video; we wanted them to interact with the content and learn from it. Our initial ideas revolved around recording at the zoo, which would offer plenty of opportunities for interaction through various animal exhibits. However, we ultimately decided to create a tutorial that teaches people how to fish.

Our tutorial highlighted fishing spots in Boise, Idaho, and other nearby locations. My responsibility was the recording and editing of the videos, as well as some of the sound design.

The primary limitation of our project was the lack of time to refine and polish it further. Despite this, the final product was a satisfactory experience that taught users how to get a fishing license, where to fish in Boise, and the basics of fishing itself. Fishing requires patience, but it also demands enough interactivity to keep people engaged, which is why we chose it as our subject. This project not only improved my video editing and sound design skills, but it also gave me experience in delegating tasks and collaborating effectively with others.
